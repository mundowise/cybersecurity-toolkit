# Ethical Use Policy

## Purpose

SecureMessenger is built to provide private, encrypted communication for individuals who need it — journalists, whistleblowers, activists, medical professionals, legal professionals, and anyone with a legitimate need for confidential communication. Privacy is a fundamental human right.

## Acceptable Use

- Private communications between consenting parties
- Journalistic source protection and whistleblower communications
- Professional confidential communications (legal, medical, financial)
- Personal privacy and protection from surveillance
- Secure team communications for organizations
- Educational exploration of encrypted messaging protocols
- Operating your own self-hosted instance for personal or organizational use

## Unacceptable Use

- Facilitating communications in furtherance of criminal activity
- Harassment, stalking, or threatening other persons
- Coordinating violence or terrorism
- Distribution of illegal content
- Any activity that violates local, state, federal, or international law

## Privacy and the Law

End-to-end encrypted messaging is legal in most jurisdictions. However, users should be aware of:

- **Lawful intercept requirements**: Some jurisdictions require communications providers to provide lawful intercept capabilities. Self-hosted deployments may have different obligations than commercial providers. Consult local law if you operate a public-facing instance.
- **Export controls**: Cryptographic software may be subject to export regulations. Review applicable rules before distributing to users in embargoed countries.
- **Data retention laws**: Some jurisdictions impose data retention obligations on communications providers. Operators of public-facing instances should review their local obligations.

This software does not include backdoors, key escrow, or any mechanism for third-party decryption of user messages.

## Legal Framework

Users and operators must comply with all applicable laws, including but not limited to:

- **United States**: Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA); Communications Assistance for Law Enforcement Act (CALEA) — applies to covered providers, not self-hosted personal use
- **European Union**: GDPR (if personal data of EU residents is processed); ePrivacy Directive
- **United Kingdom**: Investigatory Powers Act 2016; Online Safety Act 2023
- **Canada**: PIPEDA and provincial privacy laws
- **Australia**: Assistance and Access Act 2018

## Operator Responsibility

If you deploy SecureMessenger as a public-facing service, you take on additional responsibilities:

- Publish a clear privacy policy
- Comply with data protection laws in the jurisdictions you serve
- Establish a process for responding to lawful legal requests
- Notify users of any changes to data handling practices

## Responsibility

The developers of this software assume **no liability** for misuse. Users and operators are solely responsible for ensuring their use complies with all applicable laws and regulations.

## Reporting Misuse

If you become aware of this platform being used for illegal purposes, please report it to the maintainers via the contact information in SECURITY.md.
