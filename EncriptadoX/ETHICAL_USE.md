# Ethical Use Policy

## Purpose

EncriptadoX is developed to protect personal privacy and enable secure communication. Encryption is a fundamental right and a necessary tool for journalists, activists, medical professionals, lawyers, and anyone who needs to protect sensitive information.

## Acceptable Use

- Protecting personal communications and private data
- Secure storage and transmission of sensitive information
- Journalism and source protection
- Legal and medical professional communications subject to confidentiality obligations
- Educational exploration of encryption and steganography concepts
- Personal privacy on systems you own and control

## Unacceptable Use

- Concealing evidence of criminal activity
- Facilitating illegal transactions or communications
- Evading lawful law enforcement actions (where legally required to comply)
- Any activity that violates local, state, federal, or international law

## Encryption and the Law

Encryption itself is legal in most countries. However, users should be aware of specific regulations that may apply:

- **Export controls**: The United States Export Administration Regulations (EAR) govern export of cryptographic software. Software using AES-128 with no key escrow is generally eligible for license exception ENC, but users in certain embargoed countries should verify compliance.
- **European Union**: Encryption is broadly legal. GDPR encourages encryption as a data protection measure.
- **Some jurisdictions** (France historically, Russia, China, India) have had restrictions on strong encryption or requirements to provide decryption keys to authorities. Users are responsible for understanding their local laws.

This tool does **not** include key escrow, backdoors, or any mechanism for third-party decryption.

## Legal Framework

Users must comply with all applicable laws, including but not limited to:

- **United States**: Electronic Communications Privacy Act (ECPA); Export Administration Regulations (EAR, 15 C.F.R. Part 740)
- **European Union**: GDPR Article 32 (encryption as a security measure); ePrivacy Directive
- **United Kingdom**: Investigatory Powers Act 2016 (RIPA successor; compelled decryption notices possible)
- **Canada**: Criminal Code and PIPEDA
- **Australia**: Assistance and Access Act 2018 (compelled assistance obligations for providers)

## Responsibility

The developers of this software assume **no liability** for misuse. Users are solely responsible for ensuring their use complies with all applicable laws and regulations.
